Module 6 · Sustainable Practices
examtriage.in
Renewable energy
  • Solar — sun's rays, solar panels, clean & wide
  • Wind — turbines, no emissions, open/coastal
  • Hydro — rivers/dams, renewable, disrupts aquatic life
  • Biomass — organic waste, renewable if managed
  • Geothermal — Earth's heat, volcanic regions
Non-renewable
  • Fossil fuels — coal, oil, gas → pollution + warming
  • Nuclear — uranium, low emissions, radioactive waste
By region
  • Developed — high usage, fossil fuel heavy
  • Developing — growing demand, mixed sources
  • Underdeveloped — biomass dependent, low access
Key definitions
NRMManaging land, water, forests sustainably
Env. EthicsMoral principles on relationship with nature
Resp. ConsumptionUsing resources mindfully, no env. harm
Energy AuditSystematic process to evaluate energy use
Circular EconomyReduce, reuse, recycle
Intergenerational EquityPreserving Earth for future generations
Energy audit — types
  • Preliminary — quick check, bills + appliances
  • Detailed — in-depth, data logging, cost analysis
  • Internal — org's own staff
  • External — outside agent
Key takeaway — Sustainable practices = energy availability + responsible consumption + NRM + environmental ethics + energy audits. Holistic, not isolated.
